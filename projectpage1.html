TILLY

Tilly is a responsive app providing surf forecasting, while teaching its users how to read and understand it. The vision is to make surf forecasting easy for any type of surfers (beginners to advanced ones) as well as providing an accurate tool to be use daily and on the go.

PROBLEM

Beginners & advanced surfers struggle to understand a surf forecast, correctly.

POSSIBLE SOLUTION

Days on the water are meant to be everything but stressful. To avoid these, it’s good practice to check the conditions of the wind, waves, and weather before heading out to the beach. This can be determinant in avoiding dangerous situations and better prepare for a day of surfing. Existing surf reports aren’t meeting the goal of displaying complex weather data in an accessible, easy-to-comprehend way to people who regularly engage in their favourite sport. I have taken up the challenge and have developped a tool that provides three core features:

A clear and concise display of forecast data.
An educational part to learn how to read a forecast with real-life exercices.
An alert feature to help to know when is the right time to head out surfing.

OUTCOME

New joiners and more advanced surfers can trust our product to show them how to finally crack the mystery of a forecasting report. Users can practice what they have learned once a week via weekly exercices, in the learning section of the webapp. The alerts feature allows them to live their life without having to check their app constantly, and get notified as soon as the forecast has reached their preferences.

BUT WAIT...WHAT’S A FORECAST ALREADY?

Surf forecasting is the process of using offshore swell data to predict onshore wave conditions. It is used by millions of people across the world, including professionals who put their forecasts online, meteorologists who work for news crews, and surfers all over the world. It is impossible to make an exact prediction of the surf (shape and size of breaking waves), but by knowing a few factors a good prediction can be made.

KEY INSIGHTS

Forecasting is complicated - help!

All participants think that forecasting data is too complicated and all mentioned that they are guessing what they are reading. We might have discovered our USP! Research proved that the current competition doesn’t provide the learning curve customers desperately need. When interviewed, participants said they would switch to a product who can provide more learning support and exercices. This qualitative metric amongst others shows that this feature could be a product market fit.

Decrease time spend looking at screens

As forecasting is only a prediction, participants check their phones regularly during the day and waste time. Our product shall give users the ability to set up an alert whenever the conditions are matching their preferences. Research showed that the competition implemented this new feature a few years back and customers were delighted. Although it isn’t accessible from the homepage and doesn’t provide a great and easy experience. There is a missed opportunity as well here.

Accessible information with a click

When travelling, users need a way to quickly access all information about a spot, a local shop, rentals, parking etc.. Our product should provide a clear database of accurate information, available through a well-structured search function. The value to the customer is a complete database providing all the information he requires to check the forecast. The value for business could come later on, with local shops and retail partnerships: we could display promotions banners and take a commision for any sales down from our website.

TAKEAWAYS

CHALLENGES & INSIGHTS

One of the challenges on this project was first to understand, on my own, what was a surf forecast and how to read it. In order to effectively sell the idea of a webapp helping users to understand forecasting, my designs would need to reflect how easy it is and how this product make it easy for them. Then it was a matter of displaying the data in the simplest way possible but still accurately.

FUTURE PLANS & UPDATES

The search function would be my primary focus of improvement. A refinement of the data, more graphs and subtle animations to bring that delight touch to the user. The educational portal would be my second choice to improve, with more vocabulary, games and rewards to gain trust and users retention. The notifications center, and more precisely alerts: Develop sounds for push notifications as a next stage of development.

WHAT WOULD HAVE I DONE DIFFERENTLY?

This project took me more time than I initially planned it. Why? I focused on purpose on the surfing industry, as I didn’t know anything really about it. My thinking was that if I don’t have any prior knowledge, then I have no bias and my work will be solely based on user research. It actually back fired a little bit... I wanted to do as much as features as possible from my interviews findings, I have been bogged down in too much details and functions and made it too complicated for me to follow through. This project reminded me that keeping it simple, from the beginning, is key for success. Also, testing with users as much as possible in order to avoid back and forth and waste of time.
